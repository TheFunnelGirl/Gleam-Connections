Gleam Connections —
Full Client Acquisition Funnel

A complete six-stage system to go from cold audience to retained client

The diagnosis

The problem isn't that clients aren't out there.
The problem is there's no path for them to walk in.

Right now, Gleam runs entirely on referrals and personal energy. Every new client requires someone's direct attention to close — there's no backend doing any work. This strategy builds the infrastructure that runs in the background, so the next client is easier than the last one.

The six stages

1
Attention
Getting in the room
Cold audience discovers Gleam exists. They don't know they have a marketing problem yet.
Content strategy
Trial reels — B-roll with on-screen text, no face to camera required. Hooks rooted in the dream client's actual pain: "your website is costing you sales and you don't even know it." Business relevance appears at the end, subtly.
Platforms
Instagram is the primary channel. LinkedIn is secondary for B2B-leaning clients. The referral network (current clients, Sales Academy connections) is the warmest traffic of all — treat it that way.
The Dream 100 approach
Identify 100 accounts where the ideal client already hangs out: wine industry groups, lifestyle brand pages, small business communities. Engage genuinely. Become a recognizable face before pitching anything.
Content angle that works
"We audited your marketing" green screen series — break down a real brand's ecosystem (website + email + Instagram) live. Builds authority and social proof at the same time. One piece of content does three jobs.
Stage goal
Get the right person to stop scrolling. That's it. One hook. One pattern interrupt. Done.
profile visits, follows, inbound DMs
2
Entry
Capturing the lead
Warm lead raises their hand. You get them off social and onto a list you actually own.
The bait (free lead magnet)
"We audited 10 small business Instagram accounts — here's the 5 things they all got wrong." A short PDF or carousel that proves expertise without selling anything. Delivered via ManyChat trigger or email opt-in link.
Hand-raiser content
Carousels that tease — open the loop, add specificity, end with one-word CTAs like "AUDIT" or "GAPS" to trigger an automated DM. Low friction, easy yes. The bait must attract the ideal client and actively repel everyone else.
Email capture
A clean landing page (Flodesk or Tiiny.host) tied to the free resource. The email list is the asset — not the Instagram following. Once they're on the list, you can nurture without the algorithm getting in the way.
ManyChat setup
Trigger words set up on key posts — when someone comments or DMs, they instantly receive the lead magnet. This means leads are captured even when no one is watching the phone.
Stage goal
Get their email. Turn a follower into a lead. Now you own the relationship — the algorithm doesn't.
email opt-in, lead magnet download
3
Trust
Earning the right to sell
The nurture period. Prove you understand their world before you ever ask for anything.
Email sequence — 5 emails / 10 days
Email 1 — warm welcome, set expectations, one quick win tip.
Email 2 — the real reason their marketing isn't working (name the structural gap, not a mindset problem).
Email 3 — a client story or real audit finding with tangible results.
Email 4 — the biggest mistake Gleam sees in small business marketing ecosystems.
Email 5 — soft CTA to book a discovery call or apply for the ecosystem audit.
Ongoing social content
The "We audited your marketing" series keeps working during the nurture period. Every new piece of content reinforces the same message: Gleam has seen this problem before, and they know exactly how to fix it.
What builds trust fastest
Naming their specific painful reality before offering any solution. Not "here's how to do better marketing" — but "here's what's actually happening in your business right now and why." Specificity is what makes content feel personal.
The Soap Opera principle
Each email ends on a hook that makes the next one feel unmissable. Not a cliffhanger for the sake of it — but a thread. One insight leads to another. By email 5 they should be thinking "I already know I need to talk to them."
Stage goal
By the time they see an offer, they already know Gleam understands their industry, has seen this problem before, and knows how to fix it.
email opens, replies, DMs, inbound interest
4
Conversion
The offer moment
Two paths in — low-ticket diagnostic first, or straight to discovery for warm leads. Either way, something gets paid for.
Offer A — Marketing ecosystem audit ($497–$797)
Gleam audits the brand's full ecosystem: website, email list, Instagram, Google presence. Delivers a findings document plus a 45-minute Zoom call presenting the gaps. The call ends with a natural pitch into Phase 1 or the retainer. The audit IS the sales call — they pay for the diagnosis, the next step feels obvious.
Offer B — Free discovery call (warm / referred leads)
30 minutes. Diagnose live. Show them exactly where their ecosystem is broken. Pitch Phase 1 or the retainer directly. Referrals and warm Instagram leads skip the audit and come straight here — they already have enough trust built in.
The application funnel (Brunson play)
For higher-value prospects — make them apply. "We're selective about the brands we take on as retainer clients. Apply here." This completely flips the dynamic. Gleam is the expert choosing clients, not the vendor hoping to get picked.
No free strategy sessions
This is the rule that changes everything. Gleam's time and expertise have a price. The audit replaces the free call — it delivers real value AND creates the ideal conditions to pitch the next step. Every hour of free strategy that converts to nothing is a cost, not generosity.
Stage goal
Paid commitment — either the audit or a signed retainer. No more free sessions that go nowhere.
audit purchase → retainer proposal
5
Ascension
Going deeper
Audit clients become retainer clients. Retainer clients go further. The value ladder does the selling.
Phase 1 — Foundation month ($750–$1,000)
Month 1 of the retainer. While the client works through the brand playbook workbook, Gleam fixes everything visible: bio optimization, Instagram cleanup, email list setup, website quick wins. Infrastructure before content. Feels like immediate momentum — and it is.
Full retainer — ongoing management (from $1,500/month)
Social media content, email marketing, strategy, monthly reporting. Month 2 onward, once the foundation is solid. Scope is flexible but grounded — nothing shifts without a conversation. 5 retainer clients at $1,500 = $7,500/month without adding anyone new.
Project bank hours (the natural upsell)
Website overhaul, campaign strategy, brand playbook expansion, paid ad management. These get pitched as the logical next step — not a separate sales conversation. "We noticed X while working on Y — here's what we'd do next." Upsell without selling.
The lifetime value math
One retainer client staying 12 months at $1,500 = $18k. Five of those = $90k/year. The goal isn't volume — it's depth. A handful of the right clients who stay, expand, and refer is worth more than 20 short-term engagements.
Stage goal
Maximize lifetime value per client. Keep them long enough that the results speak for themselves — and then they tell someone else.
retained clients → referrals + case studies
6
Retention
The flywheel
Happy clients become the marketing engine. Referrals feed back into Stage 1 — without a single post.
Client experience that creates referrals
Clear onboarding. Proactive communication. Always knowing what happens next. North American clients especially need to feel trust consistently — silence breeds doubt. Regular Loom updates, quick wins in Slack, monthly reports that show what actually moved.
The case study engine
Every strong client result becomes content. "We took a wine brand from zero content strategy to [result] in 90 days." These fuel Stage 1 reels and Stage 3 email sequences. The work Gleam does for clients IS the marketing. Nothing extra required.
Referral system
Ask at the 60-day mark — when momentum is highest and the client is already proud of what's moving. Make it easy: one email template they can forward. Consider a referral incentive (a free project hour, a month of a reduced rate) for clients who bring in paying work.
Why this matters most
Referrals bypass Stages 1 and 2 entirely. They arrive at Stage 3 or 4 already warm. This is how you wake up with leads without publishing every single day. The funnel feeds itself — but only if Stage 6 is built properly.
Stage goal
Make the funnel self-filling. Every client should make the next one easier to find.

The Brunson principle

Whoever can afford to spend the most to acquire a client wins. A business with one offer can spend $10 to acquire a client. A business with a full value ladder can spend $300 — because they know the lifetime value of a 12-month retainer client is $18,000+.

The audit isn't a cost. It's a lead conversion tool that pays for itself the moment the client signs a retainer. The free magnet isn't charity — it's the bait that gets the right person into the room. Every rung of the ladder is a yes that makes the next yes easier.
