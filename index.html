The dream client

The brand has more potential than
its current marketing is creating.

Client 1 — Emerging founder
2+ years in, ready to grow properly
She has a real product, real customers, real passion — wine, functional beverages, lifestyle food. She's building something people genuinely love. But her marketing is scattered and pieced together. She's doing the posts, sending the occasional email, updating the website here and there — none of it connects. She's not a marketer. She just doesn't know what to prioritize, and she's tired of carrying it all alone.
Client 2 — Established brand founder
Momentum exists. The right partner doesn't.
She has revenue, recognition, momentum. But her marketing support has been executing tasks, not thinking. She needs a strategic partner who looks at the full picture, spots what's being missed, and cares about the brand the way she does. She doesn't want someone asking "what do you want us to make?" She wants someone asking "what are you trying to build, and what's getting in the way?"
The conversation already happening in her head
"I know this could work. I just don't know if I'm making the right moves."  ·  "We need to be more consistent — but consistent with what, exactly?"  ·  "I don't want to spend money on the wrong thing again."  ·  "I want someone who actually gets it and cares."

The six stages

1
Attention
Getting in the room
Cold audience discovers Gleam. They think they have a content problem. The content leads with their reality, not Gleam's.
Trial reels — the format
B-roll with on-screen text only. No face to camera required. The hook names the painful reality first — what's going wrong in their marketing right now. Business relevance appears at the end, quietly. Every reel has one job: make the right person stop scrolling.
"Things That Go Wrong" series
The strongest ongoing content angle. Each reel names one specific thing that happens when a brand is marketing without strategy:
  • You post consistently for months and nothing moves
  • Your engagement is good but nobody's buying
  • Website visitors who leave without doing anything
  • Emails that go out but nobody clicks
  • Beautiful content that doesn't say anything specific enough to convert
  • Followers who are never going to be customers
Green screen / ecosystem reviews
Pull up a real brand's Instagram, website, or email (anonymized or with consent) and walk through what Gleam notices. What the content is doing. What the customer journey is missing. What one thing would change immediately. This builds authority faster than anything else — it shows the thinking, not just the results.
The core tension Gleam speaks to
"I'll just handle our marketing myself" — and the slow realization that DIY marketing without strategy produces beautiful content that goes nowhere. Gleam is not selling against agencies. Gleam is speaking to the founder who is doing it herself while quietly knowing something isn't connecting.
Stage goal
Get the right person to stop scrolling. One hook. One pattern interrupt. That's it.
profile visits, follows, saves, inbound DMs
2
Entry
Capturing the lead
Warm lead raises their hand. They get off social and onto a list Gleam actually owns.
Option A — Lead magnet
A short PDF: "The 5 things we find wrong in almost every feel-good brand's marketing ecosystem." Delivered via a ManyChat trigger word (comment "GAPS" or "AUDIT") or a direct opt-in page. They sign up with their email — Gleam uses their existing email platform or sets up one of the platforms Gleam recommends. Evergreen. Works on any post, any time.
Option B — Taster comment audit
A carousel that opens a loop about what's breaking in most brand ecosystems, ending with: "Comment AUDIT and I'll look at your Instagram and tell you the one thing I'd change." ManyChat fires a DM asking for their handle and best email. Gleam sends a short, specific, personalized observation — delivered to their inbox. High conversion because it's specific to them.
Option C — "We'll audit your Instagram" page
A standalone opt-in page: "Tell us where to send it." They submit their email, Gleam sends a short specific observation on their profile. Slightly more manual — but highest quality leads. Only genuinely curious founders complete it.
The non-negotiable
All three routes capture the email. Social media is borrowed land. The email list is what Gleam owns — and the platform used is either what the client already has or one of the platforms Gleam recommends, set up properly from the start.
Stage goal
Get the email. Turn a follower into a lead. Now the relationship exists outside the algorithm.
email opt-in, lead magnet download, taster audit request
3
Trust
Earning the right to sell
Repetition across multiple touchpoints. By the time they see an offer, Gleam already feels like the team that understands their world.
Email sequence — 5 emails / 10 days
Email 1 — Welcome + one real thing. Thank them. Tell them what's coming. Give one specific, useful observation about the most common mistake in their type of brand. Warm, direct, specific — not generic.

Email 2 — Name the real problem. "The reason your marketing isn't working isn't because you're not posting enough." Walk through the difference between marketing tasks and marketing strategy — where every piece of content has a job to do. Make her feel seen, not lectured.

Email 3 — What a connected ecosystem looks like. No jargon. Show what a feel-good brand's full marketing looks like when it's working — discovery to first purchase to repeat customer. Where most brands fall off. Use a real example or composite.

Email 4 — A story from the work. A client story. What the brand looked like before. What Gleam found. What changed. Specific details. No pitch — just proof.

Email 5 — A soft invitation. "If any of this sounds familiar, here's what working with Gleam looks like." Both the discovery call and the ecosystem audit introduced as options. PS line handles the most common objection.
Biweekly newsletter
Sent every two weeks to the email list. The formula is non-negotiable: 90% storytelling, 10% offer — and the offer is never the focus, it's the logical next step that emerges naturally from the story.

Each edition opens with a hook that pulls the reader in — a real moment, a behind-the-scenes observation, something that happened with a brand that made Gleam think. The story does all the trust-building work. The offer at the end is quiet and obvious: "If you want this kind of thinking behind your brand, here's how to work with us."

This is what keeps Gleam top of mind between content drops. The email list is the asset. The newsletter is what makes it worth being on.
Ongoing social content
The "Things That Go Wrong" series continues running throughout the nurture period. The green screen ecosystem reviews continue. Every piece reinforces the same idea: Gleam sees what others miss, and they care enough to name it clearly.
Stage goal
By the time they see an offer, they already feel like Gleam understands their world, has seen their situation before, and knows what to do about it.
email opens, replies, inbound DMs, profile visits
4
Conversion
The offer moment
Two paths in — both lead to the same place. A paid commitment. No free strategy sessions that go nowhere.
Path A — Discovery call (warm / referred)
30 minutes. Gleam listens, asks the right questions, names what it's hearing. The call ends with a clear recommendation: either move straight into the application funnel (clear fit, established brand) — or do the ecosystem audit first. Gleam frames the audit as: "I want to make sure Phase 1 is built on what we actually find, not what we assume today." The audit then becomes the recommended next step, not a gate.
Path B — Marketing ecosystem audit ($597)
For leads who want to see the thinking before committing, or where the full picture makes Phase 1 significantly stronger.
  • Step 1 — Intake call (30 min): The founder shares where things feel off, what they've tried, what they want to grow.
  • Step 2 — Access + review: Gleam goes deep — website (journey, copy, conversion), email (sequences, health, what's missing), Instagram (bio, strategy, whether content has a job to do), full customer path from discovery to purchase.
  • Step 3 — Findings report: A clear Google Doc — what's working, what's not, the gaps in the customer journey, and what to fix first.
  • Step 4 — Findings call: Gleam presents live. The framing: these gaps are costing sales and causing customers to drop off. This is exactly what Gleam addresses in the retainer — and here's the tier that makes sense for where your brand is right now.
Both paths → Application funnel
Whether the lead came through the discovery call or the findings call, the next step is identical: a short, intentional application.

Email 1 (same day): "Based on everything we looked at today, I genuinely think Gleam can move the needle for your brand. We're selective about who we take on — here's the application if you'd like to explore working together properly."

Email 2 (3 days later, no response): Warm, no pressure. An open door to ask questions before deciding.

The application is short — brand name, current stage, goals, when they want to start. Gleam reviews and responds within 48 hours. Gleam is the strategic partner choosing carefully — not the agency hoping to be chosen.
Stage goal
A signed retainer agreement. The audit either already happened, is included in the retainer, or is included as a bonus for full 6-month payment. Either way — it always happens before Phase 1 begins.
application submitted → retainer signed
5
Ascension
The retainer — going deep
Phase 1 is the foundation. Everything after it is built on what was found and built here.
Phase 1 — Gleam Signature Start (Month 1)
The first month of the retainer. Same price as the ongoing retainer — not a separate charge. Two things run at the same time:

The foundation work (brand deep dive): The Gleam brand playbook is built here through workbooks and deep-dive sessions. This is non-negotiable — Gleam doesn't start creating content just because there's a brief and a budget. The playbook is what ensures every piece of content, every email, every post has a specific job to do and is rooted in what makes this brand genuinely different. The playbook is completed by the end of month 1.

Gap implementation (from the audit): Simultaneously, Gleam implements the most urgent fixes found in the audit. Instagram bio and profile optimization. Email platform setup or improvements using the existing platform or one Gleam recommends. Homepage copy and flow improvements — note: copy refinements and fluidity improvements are within scope; a full homepage redesign or rebuild is scoped separately as an add-on. Welcome sequence drafted. Customer journey mapped end to end.
From month 2 — content creation begins
Once Phase 1 is complete — playbook done, gaps fixed, foundation solid — content creation starts. Posts, newsletters, templates. Visual brand assets either refined if something exists, or built from the playbook outputs if starting fresh. Everything created from month 2 onward has a strategy behind it and a job to do. Deliverables follow the tier agreed at the findings call:

  • Social media content (posts, carousels, reels — per tier)
  • Email newsletters (per tier)
  • Monthly strategy and reporting
  • VA support — 5 hours/month included
  • Project bank — 5 hours/month included
  • Additional hours scoped and quoted as add-ons
Stage goal
By the end of month 1, the brand has a playbook, a fixed infrastructure, and a clear path for everything that follows. From month 2, the marketing engine runs — and every piece of it has a purpose.
Commitment
Minimum 6-month retainer. Meaningful marketing results don't happen in 30 days, and Gleam is not here to tick boxes and disappear. The 6-month minimum is also what Gleam needs to do the work properly — and what gives clients the runway to actually see it working.
retained clients → results → referrals + case studies
6
Retention
The flywheel
Happy clients become the marketing engine. Referrals feed back to Stage 1 — without a single extra post.
What retains clients
Clients stay when they feel consistently informed, supported, and proud of what's happening. Not just good work — consistent presence. The experience of working with Gleam should remove stress, not add it.
  • Clear onboarding from day one — first deliverable, check-in rhythm, month 1 roadmap
  • Proactive communication — bring updates to them, don't wait to be asked
  • Regular wins highlighted — make sure the client sees and celebrates what's moving
  • Monthly reports showing real data, not vanity metrics
  • Quarterly alignment — are we still pointed at the right goals?
Case study engine
Every strong result is a content asset. One specific client story — with real before/after details and a founder quote — does more work than a month of generic posts. Gleam asks for case study sign-off at the 60-day mark, when momentum is already visible and the client is proud of what's shifted. These stories go directly into Stage 3 emails, Stage 1 reels, and the application page.
Referral system
Ask at 60 days. Energy is highest, results are visible, the client genuinely wants to talk about the work. Make it easy — a template they can forward, a specific ask. A small thank-you for successful introductions makes it feel reciprocal, not transactional. Referrals arrive at Stage 3 or 4 already trusting Gleam.
Why this matters most
Referrals bypass Stages 1 and 2 entirely. They arrive warm, pre-sold, and ready for a real conversation. This is how the funnel feeds itself — and how Gleam wakes up with leads without having to generate every single one from scratch.
Stage goal
Every client makes the next one easier to find. The work Gleam does for clients becomes the proof that brings new ones in.
